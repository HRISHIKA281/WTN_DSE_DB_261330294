Question 1: EMPLOYEE Table (EMPNO, ENAME, SAL, DEPTNO, DNAME,
LOC)
Current State: 1st Normal Form (1NF)
To eliminate the transitive dependency, the table is split into two:
• EMPLOYEE_DETAILS (EMPNO, ENAME, SAL, DEPTNO)
• DEPARTMENT (DEPTNO, DNAME, LOC)

Question 2: STUDENT Table (ROLLNO, NAME, AGE, EXAM, MARKS, GRADE)
Current State: 1st Normal Form (1NF)
Solution (Decomposition to 2nd Normal Form):
To eliminate partial dependencies, we decompose into two tables:
• STUDENT_PROFILE (ROLLNO, NAME, AGE)
• EXAM_PERFORMANCE (ROLLNO, EXAM, MARKS, GRADE)

Question 3: EMPLOYEE-PROJECT Table (EMPNO, PROJECT_NO,
NO_OF_DAYS, CUSTOMERNAME)
Primary Key: Composite Key (EMPNO, PROJECT_NO)
Problem: The table suffers from a Partial Dependency (a 2NF violation). The
CUSTOMERNAME depends solely on the PROJECT_NO, regardless of which
employee is working on it (PROJECT_NO → CUSTOMERNAME). This causes data
redundancy and anomalies.
Solution (Decomposition to 2nd Normal Form):
Separate the partial dependency out into a clean lookup architecture:
• PROJECT_CUSTOMER (PROJECT_NO, CUSTOMERNAME)
• EMPLOYEE_WORK_LOG (EMPNO, PROJECT_NO, NO_OF_DAYS)
